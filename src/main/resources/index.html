Diseases we track

Measles

Highly contagious viral disease that can cause serious complications. We track measles-containing vaccine (MCV) coverage worldwide.

Pertussis (Whooping Cough)

Dangerous respiratory disease, especially for infants. We monitor DTP vaccine coverage (diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis).

Rubella (German Measles)

Can cause severe birth defects during pregnancy. We track rubella vaccine coverage to prevent congenital rubella syndrome.

Why vaccines matter

Vaccines save 2-3 million lives annually by training our immune systems to fight diseases. When 90-95% of a community is vaccinated, "herd immunity" protects everyone—including those who cannot be vaccinated.

Since 2000, measles deaths have decreased by over 80% globally through vaccination. Explore our data to see vaccination trends and their impact on disease prevention.
